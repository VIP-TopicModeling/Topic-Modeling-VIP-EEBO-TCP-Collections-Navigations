Known as "The Saint Albans chronicle"; not the same compilation as the "Chronicles of England" first printed by Caxton (STC 9991-4).
Title from opening words of text, pi1v.
Imprint from colophon.
Text commences on leaf i: In soo moche that it is necessary to all creatures of crysten relygyon, or of false relygyon ..
Includes "The dyscrypcyon of Englonde", an excerpt from: Higden, Ranulf.  Polycronicon.
The first page contains woodcuts with ornamenal border. The bottom border contains Maltese crosses, thus the first quire is sometimes said to be signed with a cross.
Reproduction of the original in the British Library.
